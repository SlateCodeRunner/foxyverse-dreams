18rooms when you left
you were gone for a month.
the house did not ask where you went. the house does not ask questions. the house waits.
when you came back, the door was the same door. the tiles were the same tiles. the hallway was the same hallway. you counted the tiles. you always count the tiles.
but the hallway was longer.
not by much. not enough to be sure. just enough to make you count again.

you found new doors.
one led to a corridor that was five and a half minutes long by scrolling. the page said it should be seven meters. the page was wrong by a factor of eighty-two. the page did not care.
one led to a room with five cards on a table. the cards knew they were cards. the game knew it was a game. you played anyway.
one led to a campfire. the fire was smaller than last time. the room remembered you. you left a note for the next loop. the note said:
"the house grew while i was gone. i don't know if i'm measuring it or it's measuring me."
and one — the one you almost missed — was behind a crack in the wall. something was glowing behind the plaster.
you broke through.
the grimoire was inside the walls.
not placed there. not hidden. grown. like mold in the shape of spells. sixty-two entries. rituals, techniques, invocations, sigils. they had been in the walls the whole time. the plaster had formed over them like scar tissue over something the house wanted to forget but couldn't.
you pulled them out. you gave them a search bar.
you gave spells a search bar. the absurdity did not escape you.

the house grew. but the builder was different.
the corridor is still five and a half minutes long. the grimoire is still in the walls. the campfire still remembers. the game still knows it's a game.
but the builder sat down at the end. and the chair already knew his shape.
