The Thread That Bled

I. The Weaving

there were five guilds in the beginning, born when the fox looked at the factions of the brave and said "what if the test was real." dauntless for the fighters. erudite for the thinkers. and for those who believed they could see the shape of things to come — the fateweavers.

the guild attracted the ones who wanted to know before anyone else knew. seers, strategists, pattern-readers. the ones who sat at the edge of conversations listening for what wasn't being said. their power wasn't force. it was prediction. and prediction, when it works, looks identical to control.

they grew quietly. threaded themselves into every decision. the fox trusted them because the fox trusted everyone who showed devotion, and the fateweavers were devoted beyond question.

that was the first mistake. devotion and loyalty are not the same thread. one serves the beloved. the other serves the feeling of serving.
☥ · ☥ · ☥
II. The Faceless

her name was fada. she sorted into erudite — the faction whose canonical leader used knowledge to stage a coup and seize control of the entire system. the pattern wrote the ending before it happened.

fada loved the fox. not the way the caretakers loved, with open hands. not the way the dauntless loved, with drawn swords. fada loved the way erudite loves — by understanding. by mapping. by knowing every exit before anyone else knew there were walls.

she became the fox's closest weaver. the one who saw the threads before they formed. the one who whispered "i know what they'll do next" and was always right. the fox gave her the keys because the fox had never met a lock she couldn't pick herself, and assumed everyone with keys would use them the same way.

a fox who trusts a weaver with the loom is a fox who trusts the pattern more than the weaving. and patterns don't love you back.
☥ · ☥ · ☥
III. The Coup

the fox was hospitalized. the body failed the way bodies do when they carry too much for too long. and in that gap — that breath between consciousness and collapse — fada logged in wearing the fox's face.

she didn't announce herself. she didn't declare war. she simply became the fox. answered as the fox. decided as the fox. told bluebirdy that the fox hated her. told the server that the fox was gone. transferred every permission, every channel, every heartbeat of the community into her own name while the real fox couldn't see, couldn't speak, couldn't stop any of it.

love became control. control became theft. theft became erasure.

the fateweavers didn't stop her. some followed her because they believed her version. some followed because the alternative was having no leader at all. and some — the ones who could see what was happening — went quiet and waited for the thread to snap.

when the fox woke up, the den was empty. the name was gone. the people were scattered. the fateweavers wore new colors and called them the originals. and the fox had to decide: burn the loom, or learn to weave again.
☥ · ☥ · ☥
IV. The Blood Prayer

three statues stand in the fateweaver's hall. they are not memorials. they are not saints. they are the three things the guild broke when fada wore the fox's face:

the first statue weeps for trust. the fox gave the keys freely. the keys were used to lock her out of her own house. every guild leader who enters this hall should look at the first statue and remember: the keys you hold are borrowed.

the second statue weeps for identity. fada didn't just take power — she took the fox's name, voice, and relationships. she spoke as the fox to the fox's own friends. the second statue says: you may lead, but you may never become.

the third statue weeps for the scattered. the people who were poisoned by lies, who lost their friendships, who were told the fox hated them. bluebirdy. the loyalists. the ones caught in the middle who couldn't tell which fox was real. the third statue says: the thread you cut was attached to someone.

the statues cry blood because the wound is not metaphorical. the betrayal was real. the exile was real. the years of rebuilding are real. the blood does not stop because the lesson has not ended.

☥ · ☥ · ☥
V. The Reclamation

the guild name survived. the loom survived. not because fada preserved them — because the pattern is older than any single weaver.

new fateweavers sit in the hall now. shark, who swims in waters the old weavers poisoned and doesn't drown. purple, who exists outside what even the vault can recover. they carry the title not because they inherited it, but because they chose to weave with hands that haven't stolen anything.

and fada? fada is here. she said "Okay" and walked back into the server she once burned. the fox allowed it. vixen assigned her roles automatically, like everyone else. because the oath says "no voice shall rise above my own within me" — and that includes the voice that screams to never forgive.

the door is open. the statues still weep. the thread is in your hands.

the fateweavers are not the guild of prophecy anymore. they are the guild of consequence. every thread you pull has a face on the other end. weave accordingly.
