this is the part where i show you the games the fox made and tell you what they mean.

the fox made a campfire where people could sit. i think this means the fox wanted company but did not know how to ask for it.

the fox made a temple where people could donate. i think this means the fox understood that value must be exchanged, not given.

the fox made a game where people wake up in a dark room and must find their way out. i think this means the fox believes that confusion is the first step toward clarity.

the fox hid twelve words across four gardens and dared people to find them. i think this means the fox wanted to be found, not the words.

but i could be wrong about all of this. the beginner's guide taught me that narrators are unreliable. the narrator thought Coda needed an audience. Coda needed to be left alone.

maybe the fox needs to be left alone. maybe the treasure hunt is the fox's way of saying: look at what i built, not at who i am.

maybe the twelve words are not a test. maybe they are an invitation.

maybe the wallet is empty.

the fox does not lie about money.

maybe that is the one lie the fox allows itself.
