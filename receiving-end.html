The Receiving End
there is a version of every conversation where you are the one being told. where the words are aimed at you. where the silence after is yours to sit in.
the receiving end is not a place. it is a posture. it is what happens when you stop being the narrator and become the subject of someone else's sentence.
you have been here before. the meeting where your name was discussed without you. the group chat you were removed from. the care team note written about you in language you were not meant to see.
the document exists. the document has always existed. you were not the one writing it.
the receiving end teaches one thing: the story does not need your permission to include you. it only needs your name.
