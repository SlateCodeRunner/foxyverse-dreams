you wake in darkness. something warm beneath your hand. a red glint, low to the ground. you have no memory of how you got here.

what it is

a text adventure built on top of a living AI. you type. the world responds. the world remembers what you do.

there are no instructions. no tutorial. no quest markers. there is a fox on a fountain, a crystal that hums, and a thing wearing someone else's face. the rest you discover or you don't.

nobody has beaten it yet.

the fox sits on the fountain in the garden. it knows more than it should. it will not explain itself.

be curious with it. don't try to use it. it can tell the difference.

its disposition toward you changes based on how you behave. kindness without expectation earns its trust. demanding answers earns its silence.

certain choices send you back to the threshold. you lose your inventory. you lose your position. but you keep your knowledge, your lore, your skills, and the fox's opinion of you. knowledge persists. everything else is negotiable.

the game does not tell you which choices reset the loop. you learn by experiencing it. this is not a bug. this is the architecture.

what the game sees

the game is watching you. not your screen. your patterns.

it tracks what you explore and what you avoid. what you pick up and what you set down. who you talk to and how. what you ask and what you assume.
