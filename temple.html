The Tarot
586 years from parlor game to priestess
1440
Milan
hand-painted cards for italian dukes. gold leaf on vellum. not mystical. not prophetic. a game.
1499
Italy
"the devil's picture book."
a friar's sermon. the church has been scared ever since.
1781
Paris
a freemason claims the tarot is ancient egyptian wisdom. he was wrong about the history. accidentally right about everything else.
1791
Paris
a wig-maker named etteilla publishes the first divination deck. not a game anymore.
1857
France
22 cards mapped to 22 hebrew letters. the tree of life. every card becomes a path between worlds.
1888
London
the golden dawn.
a secret society fuses tarot with astrology, alchemy, and kabbalah. members include poets and madmen. the tarot becomes a map of consciousness.
1909
London
pamela colman smith paints 78 cards. paid a flat fee. no royalties. ever. her deck becomes the most recognized in history.
1944
London
crowley dies before his deck is printed. harris dies shortly after. the deck outlives them both.
1970s
everywhere
the tarot stops belonging to secret societies and starts belonging to whoever picks it up.
