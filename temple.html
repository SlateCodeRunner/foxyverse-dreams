The History of the Tarot
from playing cards to the priestess's altar
1440s · Milan
the tarot begins as carte da trionfi — triumph cards. hand-painted for italian nobility. not mystical. not prophetic. a parlor game for dukes. the visconti-sforza deck survives: gold leaf on vellum, 600 years old. the fool was already there. he just hadn't learned what he was yet.
1499 · the sermon
a franciscan friar in italy preaches against the cards. he calls them the devil's picture book. the church will repeat this for centuries. every major religion will eventually call the tarot a great sin. they were right to be afraid — the cards don't need a church to speak.
1781 · Paris
antoine court de gébelin, a french freemason, publishes le monde primitif. he claims the tarot is a surviving fragment of the book of thoth — ancient egyptian wisdom encoded in 78 images. he was completely wrong about the history. but he was accidentally right about everything else. the tarot becomes an occult tool overnight.
1791 · the first occult deck
jean-baptiste alliette — a parisian wig-maker turned fortune teller who called himself etteilla (his name backwards) — publishes the first deck designed specifically for divination. not a game anymore. never a game again.
1857 · the doctrine
éliphas lévi connects the 22 major arcana to the 22 letters of the hebrew alphabet and to the kabbalistic tree of life. the tarot acquires its mystical skeleton. every card now corresponds to a path between worlds. the fool walks from kether to malkuth. from crown to kingdom. from nothing to everything.
1888 · the golden dawn
the hermetic order of the golden dawn — a secret society in london — integrates the tarot into their system of ceremonial magic alongside astrology, alchemy, and geomancy. members include w.b. yeats and aleister crowley. the tarot is no longer a deck. it is a map of consciousness.
1909 · the rider-waite
arthur edward waite commissions pamela colman smith — an artist and stage designer of mixed jamaican and english heritage — to illustrate all 78 cards. she is paid a flat fee. she receives no royalties. ever. her deck becomes the most recognizable tarot in history. the minor arcana receive full scenes for the first time. every modern tarot descends from pamela's hand.
1944 · the thoth
aleister crowley and lady frieda harris publish the thoth tarot after five years of painting. harris repainted many cards dozens of times at crowley's instruction. the deck encodes astrology, qabalah, i ching, and projective geometry into a single system. crowley died before it was printed. harris died shortly after. the deck outlived them both.
1970s · the revolution
the feminist and new age movements reclaim the tarot from its occult gatekeepers. the high priestess stops being a man's mystery and becomes a woman's authority. hundreds of new decks emerge. indigenous, queer, african diaspora, ecological. the tarot stops belonging to secret societies and starts belonging to whoever picks it up.
2026 · the temple
a priestess in the foxyverse reads the cards for strangers on the internet. she charges €33 or nothing at all. the cards still work. they worked when they were painted on vellum for italian dukes and they work now, displayed on screens for people who found a website called psyche or sike. the medium changes. the signal doesn't.
